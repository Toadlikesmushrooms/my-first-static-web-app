North Shore Beaches

The North Shore of Auckland is home to a series of sandy bays that stretch from Cheltenham to Torbay. The suburbs of Auckland's North Shore have a sunny seaside flavor, and there are more than ten attractive sandy beaches for sunbathing, walking, and swimming . Many of New Zealand's successful international sailors began their careers as youngsters at North Shore yacht clubs . You'll see white triangles of sail decorating the bays on a breezy day. You'll also notice kayakers touring the coastline, rounding the tall sandstone headlands between one beach and the next. Offshore, the tree-covered volcanic cone of Rangitoto Island provides a uniquely Auckland backdrop and a fascinating destination for more experienced paddlers and sailors . When easterly winds blow onshore, a swell develops across the outer Hauraki Gulf bringing surf to these normally tranquil beaches. Surfing enthusiasts can enjoy the waves at these beaches .

Milford Beach

Milford Beach is located in Auckland, New Zealand. It is a popular swimming beach with picturesque views of Rangitoto Island . The beach is also home to a nautical-themed playground with pirate ship, rope climbing, and balance beams, which is loved by kids of all ages . You can explore the coastline from the northern end of Milford Beach to the southern end of Takapuna Beach, taking in the area's rock pools, beaches, and scenery . The walk starts at Milford Reserve and takes you along the shoreline in places, with uninterrupted views of the Hauraki Gulf and Rangitoto Island . There are a number of good swimming beaches along the way, from secluded Thorne Bay to the p≈çhutukawa-lined Takapuna Beach . The Milford-Takapuna walk is best at low tide as the rocks are exposed and the path is dry . The walk is suitable for all fitness levels, although there are some parts where the path is rocky and rough, and can be slippery . Public transport is available from Milford Reserve end - walk over the pedestrian bridge or down Omana Road to catch a bus on Inga Road. From Takapuna Beach end - the nearest buses are on Hurstmere and Lake roads .

Narrow Nech beach

Narrow Neck Beach is a lovely stretch of golden sand located in Auckland, New Zealand . It offers picturesque views of Rangitoto Island and the Hauraki Gulf . The beach is a favorite among locals who enjoy walking, kayaking, and fishing in the picture-postcard setting . There's also a boat ramp for launching smaller vessels . When the tide is out, it's possible to walk around to Cheltenham Beach, another pretty beach that leads up to North Head Historic Reserve . The facilities by the beach include a cafe, seating areas, a BBQ area, a small playground, and toilets . A short stroll from the beach brings you to the Fort Takapuna Historical Reserve, featuring remnants from its military past and incredible views of the Hauraki Gulf . The beach is accessible by car or public transport.
